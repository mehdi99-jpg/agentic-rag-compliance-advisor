# OECD Principles on Artificial Intelligence - Reference Summary

Originally adopted in May 2019 and updated in 2024, the OECD (Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development) AI Principles serve as an international benchmark for the responsible and trustworthy development of artificial intelligence.

---

## 1. Value-Based Principles for Trustworthy AI

AI actors should respect the following five principles throughout the AI system lifecycle:

### Principle 1.1: Inclusive Growth, Sustainable Development, and Well-being
* AI should be developed and used to benefit people and the planet by fostering inclusive growth, sustainable development, and overall well-being.
* AI systems should reduce inequality, protect the environment, and support human flourishing.

### Principle 1.2: Respect for Human-Centered Values and Fairness
* AI systems must respect human rights, democracy, rule of law, and diversity.
* AI actors should implement safeguards (e.g., human-in-the-loop checks) to ensure fairness, protect privacy, and prevent unlawful discrimination or bias.

### Principle 1.3: Transparency and Explainability
* AI systems must be transparent and explainable.
* AI actors must provide clear, understandable information about how the AI operates, how decisions are reached, and who is responsible.
* This allows individuals affected by an AI system's output to understand the rationale and challenge the results if necessary.

### Principle 1.4: Robustness, Security, and Safety
* AI systems must be robust, secure, and safe throughout their entire lifecycle.
* Potential safety and security risks (including cyber threats and unintended behaviors) must be continuously assessed, managed, and mitigated.
* AI systems should include fail-safe mechanisms and be resilient to manipulation.

### Principle 1.5: Accountability
* AI actors are accountable for the proper functioning of AI systems and for compliance with the principles above.
* Organizations and individuals who develop, deploy, or operate AI must take responsibility for its real-world impacts.

## 2. Policy Recommendations for Governments

The OECD recommends that governments implement policies to support trustworthy AI through five key action pillars:

1. **Investing in AI Research & Development**: Support long-term investment in public and private research to advance trustworthy AI systems and explore new technologies.
2. **Fostering a Digital Ecosystem**: Provide open access to high-quality datasets, infrastructure, and tools while respecting privacy and intellectual property rights.
3. **Shaping an Enabling Policy Environment**: Create flexible policy frameworks, sandbox testing environments, and regulations that encourage innovation while protecting human rights.
4. **Building Human Capacity**: Prepare the workforce for labor market transitions by investing in education, training, and reskilling programs for AI-related jobs.
5. **International Co-operation**: Collaborate globally to share best practices, align standards, and ensure the safe development of AI systems across borders.
