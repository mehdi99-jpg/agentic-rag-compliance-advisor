# EU Artificial Intelligence Act (EU AI Act) - Reference Summary

The European Union Artificial Intelligence Act (EU AI Act) is the world's first comprehensive horizontal legal framework for regulating AI. It follows a risk-based approach, categorizing AI applications based on their potential to cause harm.

---

## 1. AI Risk Classifications

### A. Unacceptable Risk (Prohibited AI Systems)
AI systems that pose a threat to people's safety, livelihoods, and rights are strictly prohibited.
* **Prohibited Practices**:
  * **Cognitive Behavioral Manipulation**: Systems that subliminally manipulate behavior, leading to physical or psychological harm.
  * **Social Scoring**: Classifying individuals based on social behavior, socio-economic status, or personal traits.
  * **Real-time Biometric Identification**: Use of remote biometric identification in publicly accessible spaces by law enforcement (with very narrow exceptions).
  * **Biometric Categorization**: Categorizing natural persons based on sensitive characteristics (political opinions, religious beliefs, sexual orientation, race).
  * **Predictive Policing**: Assessing individuals to predict criminal behavior based on profiling or traits.
  * **Emotion Recognition**: Using AI to detect emotions in workplace or educational environments (except for safety/medical reasons).

### B. High-Risk AI Systems
AI systems that negatively impact safety or fundamental rights. These are permitted but subject to strict compliance obligations.
* **Categories**:
  * **Biometrics**: Systems used for biometric identification, verification, or categorization.
  * **Critical Infrastructure**: AI in road traffic, water, gas, heating, or electricity networks.
  * **Education and Vocational Training**: AI used for student assessment, admissions, or determining access to learning programs.
  * **Employment and Worker Management**: AI for recruitment (sorting CVs), promotions, task allocation, or performance monitoring.
  * **Essential Services**: Credit scoring, credit evaluation, and prioritizing emergency response services (fire, medical, police).
  * **Law Enforcement**: AI used for assessing evidence credibility, risk profiling of suspects, or polygraph testing.
  * **Migration, Asylum, and Border Control**: Verification of travel documents, visa processing, or security risk profiling.
  * **Administration of Justice & Democratic Processes**: AI used by judges or courts to assist in legal interpretation and fact-finding.

### C. Limited Risk (Transparency Obligations)
AI systems with specific transparency requirements. Users must be notified they are interacting with AI.
* **Examples**: Chatbots (e.g., customer service support agents), generative systems producing text/images/video (deepfakes), and emotion recognition systems (outside education/employment).
* **Obligations**: Clearly label AI-generated content (watermarking) and notify users they are interacting with an automated system.

### D. Minimal Risk (No Specific Obligations)
AI systems representing minor or no risk to citizens.
* **Examples**: Video games, spam filters, or AI-enabled search bars.
* **Regulation**: No obligations under the AI Act, though general product safety laws still apply.

---

## 2. Compliance Obligations for High-Risk AI Systems

Providers of high-risk AI systems must meet several strict requirements before putting systems on the EU market:

1. **Risk Management System**: Establish a continuous, iterative risk management process throughout the system's lifecycle.
2. **Data Quality and Governance**: High-risk AI must use high-quality training, validation, and testing datasets that are representative, free of errors, and checked for bias.
3. **Technical Documentation**: Maintain comprehensive, up-to-date documentation explaining how the AI works to prove compliance.
4. **Record-Keeping (Logging)**: Automatic event logging to track system execution, trace operations, and monitor performance.
5. **Human Oversight**: High-risk systems must be designed to allow effective human monitoring and override capabilities (preventing automation bias).
6. **Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity**: Ensure appropriate levels of resilience against attacks, errors, or unauthorized access.

---

## 3. Financial Penalties and Fines

Fines are calculated based on global annual turnover or a fixed maximum amount, whichever is higher:

* **Non-compliance with Unacceptable/Prohibited AI Practices**:
  * Fines up to **€35,000,000 (€35 Million)** or **7% of the company's global annual turnover** of the preceding financial year, whichever is higher.
* **Non-compliance with High-Risk System Requirements or Transparency Rules**:
  * Fines up to **€15,000,000 (€15 Million)** or **3% of the company's global annual turnover** of the preceding financial year, whichever is higher.
* **Supplying Incorrect, Incomplete, or Misleading Information to Regulators**:
  * Fines up to **€7,500,000 (€7.5 Million)** or **1.5% of the company's global annual turnover** of the preceding financial year, whichever is higher.
